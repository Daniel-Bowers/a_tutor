Values ▼

  • Voluntaryism: All interactions between humans should be voluntary; violence and coercion should only be used in self-defense. This is called the Non-Aggression Principle (NAP). I only teach students who want to be taught. If I make a trip to a home to find the student does not want my help, I will leave and charge you for the trip; communication is important. As a parent, you have a lot of leverage in negotiation with your child; threats or forceful control are unnecessary.
  • Honesty: When tutoring students, I try not to help them so much that it becomes my work rather than theirs. However, some of my online spreadsheets show step-by-step how to solve a problem. When I was in college, I used a solution manual while doing my math work. I had a cycle of working a little, then checking my work against the manual. On my homework I indicated that I had done this so that my teacher could judge how much credit to give me. I recommend this to students who want to learn. Students who don't want to learn can simply copy the steps. Since unearned grades may be used to misrepresent the student's talents to a future employer—a voluntary interaction—I feel uncomfortable with this despite the fact that student may be forced to take the class, which violates the NAP. I want to help students who want to learn more than I want to avoid helping students cheat, so my spreadsheets will remain available. You should consider the character of your child before recommending this site to him or her. Regarding piano lessons, students should have legitimate copies of music.
